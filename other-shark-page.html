Random Shark Facts

Cookie cutter shark

The cookiecutter shark is one of the most interesting sharks in the ocean, and it never grows bigger than 18-20 inches (~50 cm). It gets its common name from its feeding strategy of biting off small chunks of much larger animals.

Saw shark

Check this out: sawsharks have a special tool called the "ampullae of Lorenzini" on their saw. It's like their electric field detector for spotting hidden prey.

These guys are the ninja hunters of the sea, sneaking around slowly near the bottom, blending in like pros to surprise their dinner!

Bigeye tresher shark

Bigeyes are easily recognized by their huge, upward-rotating eyes—10cm long! These eyes give them awesome vision, even in low light.

Plus, they've got grooves on their head that make them look like they're wearing a helmet. Oh, and their tail fins can be as long as their bodies! These sharks are both stylish and functional in the ocean.
